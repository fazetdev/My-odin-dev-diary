Week 1: Setup, Sync, and the Start of Style

DATE thursday 12 june 2025

Welcome to the chaotic beauty of Week 1 — where I officially kicked off my Odin Project journey with equal parts caffeine and confusion. This week was all about laying the foundation: getting my dev setup ready, battling Git (and mostly surviving), and building this very diary you're reading.

🛠️ The Setup Saga

I connected my phone and laptop into one coding unit like some kind of budget Iron Man. Between Termux, GitHub, and some very focused 2-hour laptop sprints (RIP my battery), I made sure nothing — not even dying power — could stop this dream.

📁 The Mini Diary Project Begins

Inspired by the Odin Git lessons, I created this little web diary to document every week of my learning. Not just for fun, but also to:

  • Test what I’ve learned
  • Practice real-world Git workflows
  • Track my growth in a way future-me (and maybe recruiters 👀) can look back on

🧠 What I Actually Learned

This week, I dove into the core of the Odin curriculum:

  • Git & GitHub: Cloning, committing, pushing, and learning
  • HTML Basics: Tags like p, strong, img, and a became my new besties
  • Starting CSS: A splash of style to stop my diary from looking like a Word document from 2003
